Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. It has a unique side rotation and is often described as "rolling around the Sun on its side." Uranus is known for its blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere Uranus The planet's atmosphere has a complex layered cloud structure and has the lowest minimum temperature of 49 K (−224 °C; −371 °F) out of all the Solar System's planets. It has a marked axial tilt of 82.23° with a retrograde rotation period of 17 hours and 14 minutes. This means that in an 84-Earth-year orbital period around the Sun, its poles get around 42 years of continuous sunlight, followed by 42 years of continuous darkness. has 27 natural satellite does not have any artificial satellite.
